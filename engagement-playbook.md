# Engagement Playbook

The non-content side of the system. Most LinkedIn growth in the first 90 days comes from this file, not from the posts themselves.

## The first 60 minutes after publishing (highest leverage)

LinkedIn's algorithm weights early engagement heavily. The first hour decides whether a post crosses its initial reach threshold and gets fed to a wider audience.

1. **Post at the scheduled time** (don't fiddle — see "algorithm habits" below for why edits hurt)
2. **Reply to every comment within the hour** — even a thoughtful one-liner. This signals "live conversation" to the algorithm
3. **Drop the first comment yourself** with any link, source, or "P.S." that didn't fit the post (links in the body cut reach ~40–60%)
4. **Notify 3 people** who'd genuinely care via DM with the post link and one honest sentence ("you came to mind because…"). Not a public tag dump
5. **Do not edit the post** — early edits temporarily reduce reach

## Daily 30-minute comment ritual

Comment first, post second. This is how the first 90 days of growth actually work.

**The ritual (do this before publishing your own post each day):**

1. Open your LinkedIn home feed
2. Spend 20 minutes commenting on 5–10 posts from your engagement list (below)
3. **Comment quality bar:** every comment is 2+ sentences and adds something — a counter-take, a related example, a specific question, a number
4. **No "great post!" comments.** They're invisible to LinkedIn's ranker and to the original poster's audience
5. Then publish your post

**Why this works:** LinkedIn shows your comments to the original poster's audience. A great comment on a 10K-impression post can pull more profile views than your own post does that day. You're borrowing distribution.

## DM playbook

3 new connections per day. Compounds quietly into inbound DMs by month 2.

**The opening DM template (one-line, non-pitchy):**

> "Saw your post on [specific thing]. The [specific point] resonated — we ran into the same thing with [your specific context]. Following along."

**Rules:**

- Reference one specific thing from a recent post they wrote
- Never pitch in the first DM
- Never mention what you sell unless they ask
- If they reply, send one follow-up that asks a question — then let it breathe

**Who to DM:**

- People who left thoughtful comments on your posts this week
- People you saw twice this week in your feed engaging with adjacent content
- New followers (within 48h of follow)

## Quote-react / borrow-distribution moves

When Anthropic, OpenAI, a major Claude release, or a big AI account ships news:

1. Reply within 60 minutes of their post going up
2. Your reply is its own mini-post: 2–3 sentences with a real take, not "love this"
3. Add a hot take or a numbers-backed observation
4. Their audience sees your reply in the activity feed

**This single move is the highest-leverage borrow-distribution play on LinkedIn in 2026.**

## Algorithm habits (what the platform actually rewards)

Source: 2025–26 reach analyses + creator economy data.

| Do | Don't | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Native uploads (carousels, images, videos) | Share an image of a tweet | Native = +30–50% reach |
| Links in first comment | Links in post body | Body links: -40–60% reach |
| Post at the same times each day | Random timing | Algorithm learns your "normal" reach |
| Reply to comments within 60 min | Edit the post in the first hour | Early edits temporarily demote |
| 3–5 hashtags, mix of sizes | 10+ hashtags | Hashtag stuffing is demoted |
| Tag people only when relevant | Tag 5+ people randomly | Random tagging is demoted |
| Line breaks every 1–2 sentences | Wall of text | Dwell time drops on walls |
| Post 4–7x/week | Post 14x/week | Per-post reach drops 18–32% above 7/week |

1. Run the Sunday 4pm sweep (auto). Review the summary.

## Monthly ritual (last Sunday, 30 min)

1. Read `performance-log.md` monthly rollup
2. Reweight pillars in `playbook.md` if any pillar has been <70% baseline for the month
3. Prune the engagement list — remove inactive accounts, add 2–3 new ones
4. Refill `wednesday-topic-bank.md` if it's below 8 unfilled topics
5. Audit DM funnel — how many of your inbound DMs in the last 30 days came from posts vs comments vs cold? Lean into whatever's working.

## Things that look like growth but aren't

Don't optimize for these:

- **Likes-only posts** — algorithmic noise, no DMs come from these
- **Follower count alone** — a 10K-follower account with no inbound DMs is worse than a 2K-follower account with 5/week
- **Vanity comment threads** — when 12 of the 15 comments are from your engagement pod and they all say "great post"
- **Going broad to be "relatable"** — generic posts get generic engagement; specific posts get the right people DMing

The North Star metric: **inbound DMs per week from people in your target audience.** Everything else is leading indicator.
